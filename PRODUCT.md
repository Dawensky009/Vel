# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Three audiences read the same page at once, and the design serves all three without a menu of modes.

The **families** of Delmas and greater Port-au-Prince are primary. A parent lands from a WhatsApp or Facebook share, on a mid-range Android over 3G/4G, and must understand in about twenty seconds: it's a book event, it's the 15th of August at the Palais Municipal de Delmas, entry is 500 HTG for an adult and free for children fourteen and under, and reserving happens through WhatsApp. Their job is to decide, quickly and with their kids in mind, whether to come.

**Sponsors and institutional funders** are secondary. They arrive skeptical, looking for editorial seriousness before they attach their name. They need a dignified "become a sponsor" path and a page whose restraint signals that their logo would be in good company.

**Staff and the coalition** are the third audience. The page has to make them proud enough to share it into their own networks; its visual legitimacy is part of what the project is buying.

## Product Purpose

A single-page site for Vacances en livres, a book-promotion event run by a coalition of Haitian cultural organizations, held 15 August 2026 at the Palais Municipal de Delmas. The page exists to convert families into reservations, open a credible door for sponsors, and give the coalition a public face worth standing behind. Success is reservations booked through WhatsApp before the day, sponsor conversations opened, and a page the staff circulate without being asked.

## Positioning

Not one more book fair. A neighborhood library opened to the public for a day — thirty authors signing, families leaving with books they wouldn't have bought alone — priced so that a family can actually come.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA:** Réserver — a pre-filled WhatsApp message to +509 5579-2024 (the working channel today; a Tally form replaces it when online reservations open). **Secondary:** pay on the day, on site, in gourdes or dollars.
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds:** 500 HTG, free for your kids under 14, the 15th of August in Delmas.
- **Belief ladder:** this is a real, organized event → it's for families like mine → the price is fair and my children get in free → reserving is easy and I trust who I'm paying → I book on WhatsApp.
- **Proof on hand:** the official poster (`Ressources/Annonces.jpeg`); the coalition of libraries, publishers, community libraries and teachers; concrete logistics (address, hours, contacts, payment methods). Author list, partner logos and sponsor names are pending the staff brief.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, warm, and dignified — literary without being precious. The voice is that of a well-run neighborhood library in late-afternoon light: everything in its place, nothing shouting, an invitation to sit down and read. Confident enough to use restraint as the statement. Anchored to Éditions Gallimard's text-forward calm, The New York Review of Books' strong hierarchy, and Poilâne's warm French reticence.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS landing page (Vercel/Linear gradients, hero-metric grids). Not a saturated Caribbean event poster (ribbons, waves, tropical motifs, tilted stamps). Not a Kinfolk-style over-styled magazine spread. No decorative illustration (palm, family, hibiscus), no hand-lettering, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no tracked-uppercase eyebrows, no numbered section scaffolding. Any Caribbean or literary reading should come from words and the poster, never from decoration.

## Design Principles

- **Respect the book.** Text-forward, generous measure, real hierarchy. The typography is the art direction.
- **Scannable in twenty seconds, dignified on the tenth read.** A parent gets price and date instantly; a sponsor finds seriousness on closer reading.
- **Restraint is the statement.** One warm accent (sun on CTAs), cream everywhere, navy ink. Color is rationed, never decorative.
- **Fast is a feature, not a finish.** Built for 3G/4G Haiti: content visible without JS, motion only enriches, images and fonts kept lean.
- **Borrow everything; sign only what must be yours.** Lean on real assets and proven copy; reserve craft for the parts that carry the coalition's name.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Verify contrast on every combination, especially green and sun on cream — sun is only ever a background under navy text, never text on cream. Content is fully readable without JavaScript; reveal motion only enriches an already-visible default and honors `prefers-reduced-motion`. Full keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic landmarks, descriptive alt text, French as the document language.
